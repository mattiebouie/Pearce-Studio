"drrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr...." Well, here we are then! Below is a sampling of some the work I am most proud of. This site doesn't contain everything I've done, just the ripest pickings. I've written up little bios for each project to frame them and lend a little perspective. You can see some of these projects and their process in greater depth on my Béhance profile, so be sure to check that out if ya dig that sort of thing!

Alright, nuff said. Have a look around, I'd love to hear from you if something strikes you. Enjoy!

Pearce Studio: The idea behind my personal branding was to convey a sense of old and new, the relationship between traditional art and current design, and the blending of the two as is reflected in my work: use of the hand and traditional art mediums within current design standards/trends. The icon mark, is an inkwell, a favorite tool of mine, which conveys tradition, while the custom lettering presents a modern touch.
Empowerment Through the Word Ministries: I was asked by a friend–fellow alumni-minister who lead this campus ministry to give them a identity that would to bring them unto the current, "trendy" college scene. While easily the longest name for an oragnization that I've ever worked with, which presented obvious challenges, the clear solution was a typographic-lead design with a complimenting logo.
Morning Star Baptist Church: Another project for my afore-mentioned friend. This time I was asked to redesign his church's logo. Their original "logo" (though a just menagerie of clip art) conveyed a lot of important symbolizm. I worked with a committee from the church to develop the new logo, who were adament that the dove, globe and crown remain in the new logo. Not to toot my own horn, but I'd say the revamp was an improvement.
River of Life Church: I have done a lot of design work for ROL over the years, being that it is my home church. This past year, I had the opportunity to help with rebranding the church. This included revising their old logo (which I had designed back in highschool) and creating display/screen graphics for use in the church's services and promotional materials. The church also put together a team of interior designers to refab the aesthetic of the building, so much of my work in creating display graphics cooresponded with the direction they took.
Tyda: I've done a lot of work for Tyda, a company that my father started, which began with the development of the Tyda logo and brand identity. Projects have since then moved to print and most currently, animation. For more on that, visit the animation section of my portfolio!
Langlais Tax & Accounting, LLC: The owner of this company, a fellow member of my church, was expanding his business and looking to rebrand. He wanted his business to appeal to an expanding target market and convey the values of integrity, trust and dependability, as well as personability. The concept of the ink pen conveys this careful, trustworthy character, while the overall logo presents a respectable, yet approachable, business entity.
The Intern: This is an ongoing personal project that came out of my hunt for an internship. The idea was to create a comic centered around an every-man intern as he navigates through the stereotypes and stygmatisms of the average internship and, ultimately, reaches self-discovery. What you see here are the hand-drawn elements used in a promotional poster. You can view the poster and more of the completed project on my Béhance. The comic itself is something I mull over and tinker with as time allows. More work to come!
Assorted Cartoons: More work for Tyda here. A mixture of business related and quirky cartoons from an ever growning collection featured on the Tyda website for a little comic relief between the heavy reading, product specifications and teachnical information featured on the site.
Wasted Typeface: A custom font that I developed for a school project: an infograph on global food wasted. Each letter was carefully hand drawn and then rendered and colored digitally.
The Chandelemur: One of several mythical creature I created during my internship at Gaslight Creative. The awesome people at Gaslight asked me to mockup these creatures as a possible angle for an internal project. The project ended up going a different direction in the end, but the Chandelemurs lived on... forever...
Alien Illustrations: These tedious paintings are from a series of twenty-one alien creatures that I created as part of a senior project. The project: to build a website to display a collection of information/images. I chose to make my site a fictional database for a near-future space exploration conglomerate which had discovered and documented life on another planet (I'm a nerd, I know!). You can see more of the site in the Web Design section of my portfolio. Gathered here are images of the illustrations themselves.
Traditional animation: A promotional video for my client-dad at Tyda. This is just a teaser for the actual animation, a collaborative effort with my brother, Mike Anderson. He is doing the narration, helping to write an original score and composite the final piece. The project is in its final stages and will be up soon! If you want to see it as soon as it's done, follow me on Twitter @pearcestudioM and you'll be the first to hear! Also, you should check out my brother's work on his personal site and follow him on Twitter @mrmikea!

SADSE Website: This is the site built around my alien creature illustrations. The site structure and design is quite simple; both because I spent so much time on the illustrations and back story that I had less time for the development of the site and also because a stripped-down, minimalistic design seemed to say, "hello, I'm from the future." The main feature of the website was the slider which allows the viewer to comparatively study the creatures.
